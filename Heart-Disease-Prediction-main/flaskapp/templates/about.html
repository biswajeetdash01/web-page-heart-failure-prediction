About Biomarkers

This machine learning model could help in estimating the probability of deaths caused by heart failure by taking in important features from the dataset and making predictions based on these features.

The dataset consists of 11 variables/features, and 1 output variable/target variable. Let us examine the role of each feature in determining if a person is likely to have heart failure or not:

1. Sex: gender of the patient
2. Resting ECG - resting electrocardiographic results
    • Normal: Nothing to note
    • ST: ST-T Wave abnormality
        • can range from mild symptoms to severe problems
        • signals non-normal heart beat
    • LVM: Possible or definite left ventricular hypertrophy
        • Enlarged heart's main pumping chamber
3. Chest Pain Type - chest pain type
    • ATA: Atypical angina: chest pain not related to heart
    • NAP: Non-anginal pain: typically esophageal spasms (non heart related)
    • ASY: Asymptomatic: chest pain not showing signs of disease
    • TA: Typical angina: chest pain related decrease blood supply to the heart
4. Exercise Angina:You might worry that exercising could trigger your symptoms or cause a heart attack, but the risk is low if you:build up your activity level gradually and take regular breaks. (yes/no)
5. ST slope - the slope of the peak exercise ST segment
    • Up: Upsloping: better heart rate with excercise (uncommon)
    • Down: Downslopins: signs of unhealthy heart
    • Flat: Flatsloping: minimal change (typical healthy heart)
6. Age: This variable shows the patient’s age
7. Resting Blood Pressure :resting blood pressure (in mm Hg on admission to the hospital) anything above 130-140 is typically cause for concern

8. Cholesterol - serum cholestoral in mg/dl
    • serum = LDL + HDL + .2 * triglycerides
    • above 200 is cause for concern
9. (Fasting Blood Sugar > 120 mg/dl) (1 = true; 0 = false)
    • '>126' mg/dL signals diabetes
10. Max Heart Rate- maximum heart rate achieved
11. oldpeak - ST depression induced by exercise relative to rest looks at stress of heart during excercise unhealthy heart will stress more.
